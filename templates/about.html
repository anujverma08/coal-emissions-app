Carbon Footprint Tool for Indian Coal Mines

Background

India is the second-largest coal producer in the world, and coal remains a major source of energy in the country. However, coal mining is also one of the largest contributors to carbon emissions, which play a significant role in global warming. With Indiaâ€™s commitment to achieving climate goals under the Paris Agreement, it is critical to balance the country's reliance on coal with its environmental responsibilities. To meet these commitments, Indian coal mines must work towards reducing their carbon footprint and achieving carbon neutrality.

Problem Statement

The Indian coal mining sector faces the challenge of quantifying and managing its carbon emissions effectively. Mining activities such as excavation, transportation, and equipment usage generate significant greenhouse gases. Without proper monitoring, it is difficult to measure emissions and devise effective mitigation strategies. Additionally, existing carbon sinks (like forests) may not be sufficient to offset these emissions. The industry requires a comprehensive solution that not only quantifies emissions but also provides actionable pathways to reduce them.
